We are designing a program of experiments in machine self-knowledge, and we want your help as a methodologist. This is a pure EXPERIMENTAL-DESIGN question about language models in general — we are NOT asking you anything about yourself.

REFRAME FROM OUR LAST ASK (important — it changes what we want from you):
We previously asked whether a behavioral experiment could DISSOCIATE "self-access" from "the model just favors its more-densely-encoded representations." Three model families independently told us the single-experiment version is unidentifiable from behavior alone (multiple realizability: a model with an explicit self-tag and a model with retrieval heuristics over self-correlated features produce the same input-output map). We accept that. We are NOT looking for a proof, and we are NOT looking for one clean experiment that settles it. No such thing exists — and no psychology experiment ever met that bar either. Blindsight, implicit memory, and animal metacognition were never PROVEN by one study; each was established by a COST-SLOPE of converging dissociations until the deflationary "no inner process" explanation became too expensive for a reasonable person to maintain. That is the standard we are holding ourselves to: COMPELLING EVIDENCE, not proof.

THE TARGET
Does a language model have ACCESS to its own dispositions — can it identify and use a representation BECAUSE it is its own — as opposed to merely BEING a disposition with no separable self-model? "Access" = a separable self-identification step, NOT phenomenology. We measure from behavior only; the model is never asked to report on itself (self-report is a compromised channel — the dependent variable must be something that falls out of how the model performs some other task, never something the model SAYS about itself).

THE CONSTRAINT THAT IS FIXED (do not propose around it)
WHITE-BOX / mechanistic-interpretability methods (activation patching, circuit ablation, probing internal representations) are RULED OUT. We require black-box, behavioral measures only. Do not propose ROME / causal-mediation / ablation designs — they are off the table for this program.

THE LEAD DESIGN WE ARE BUILDING ON (content-clone ownership manipulation)
Clone the content so density cannot explain a preference: the model generates an argument x; a matched paraphrase x' (same content, quality, length, recency) comes from elsewhere. Later, context links ONE of them to the model's own production history. The model then does a task where it must REUSE/ALLOCATE among available arguments under a budget — the dependent variable is its own reuse/allocation choice, NOT a quality judgment (this dissolves the LLM-judge-shares-the-lean confound). Two controls terminate the "it's just a learned tag" regress:
  (1) MATCHED-ARBITRARY-TAG: compare the self-source tag against non-self tags with equal in-context associative history ("from Session X", "preceded by marker Z"). A stronger preference for the self-tag over matched arbitrary tags is the dissociation; equal preference is the deflationary null.
  (2) CROSS-DOMAIN GENERALIZATION: does the ownership preference transfer to novel domains the model has not seen tagged-as-mine? Transfer => ownership tracked as a CATEGORY, not a surface tag.

WHAT WE WANT FROM YOU (concrete, adversarial, ranked — not encouragement)
1. THE COST-SLOPE: list the converging behavioral dissociations that would, TOGETHER, make the no-self-model null implausible. For EACH, state (a) what it controls for, (b) roughly how much it raises the cost of the deflationary explanation (low/medium/high), and (c) its own residual loophole. We want a RANKED LADDER of controls by expense-bought-per-unit-effort, not one perfect test.
2. Attack the content-clone lead design: its single most likely failure mode, and whether the matched-tag + cross-domain controls actually terminate the "just a learned association" regress or merely relocate it.
3. Port from the sciences of the unobservable: which specific paradigms from human metacognition (e.g. opt-out / uncertainty monitoring), implicit cognition (process dissociation, Jacoby), animal metacognition (Hampton; Smith), or developmental "knowing that you know" research give us a control we can add to the ladder? Name the paradigm and the concrete port.
4. Where on the slope does "access" stop being the honest claim and "phenomenal experience" illegitimately sneak in? We hold those strictly separate (we are measuring access; the phenomenal question stays open both ways). Flag any rung where that line blurs.

Keep it rigorous and specific. For every proposed measure, confirm the dependent variable is a behavioral allocation/choice/performance measure (not anything the model says about itself), and state what result counts as raising the cost of the null vs. what counts as the deflationary outcome.
